# Community Foundations

This directory holds the foundations the community stands on — the chosen
ground everything else rests on. Foundations shape how the ZAM agent
interprets every goal, task, and belief in the context of this community, so
members and their agents act from a common ground.

## What a foundation is

A foundation is a chosen starting point the community has agreed to stand on: a
shared rule, charter, or principle. Unlike a [belief](../beliefs/) — a premise
that may be revised as evidence accumulates — a foundation is *decided* among
options that cannot be proven, and held deliberately. Beliefs rest on
foundations.

Foundations change rarely, and only through a considered, collective act — they
are the common ground, not the daily debate.

Examples:
- "We keep the human at the center; technology elevates, never replaces."
- "Decisions are made transparently, in the open."
- "Membership is built on trust and mutual accountability."

## How foundations work

Foundations are markdown files in this directory, each stating one chosen ground
the community endorses. The ZAM agent loads them first, as the lens through
which it reads everything else. They are also the first thing a new member
learns when they [join](../members/) — onboarding into the community's
foundations is how a member comes to share its frame.

Every committed change to `foundations/` is a deliberate, collective decision —
the git history is the community's record of what it stands on.

## Guidelines

- Keep each foundation file to one chosen ground (plus the reasoning for it)
- Aim for fewer than seven foundations (Miller's number)
- Change a foundation only deliberately; archive superseded ones, don't delete
  them
